Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

Why not just use ChatGPT or Claude directly?

‚ñº

Great question! You absolutely can - and many people do. But here's the thing: asking "How do I use AI at my plumbing company?" to ChatGPT is like asking Google "How do I fix my life?" - the answer is too broad to be useful.

This platform does three things ChatGPT alone can't do:

  • Asks the RIGHT questions upfront (your industry, tools, budget, tech level)
  • Gives you a complete implementation plan, not just ideas
  • Helps you pitch it to your boss with actual numbers

Think of it like: ChatGPT is a brilliant friend who can answer anything. This is that friend plus a structured workbook that makes sure you actually achieve your desired goal.

How is this different from online AI courses?

‚ñº

Most AI courses teach you about AI - what it is, how it works, cool possibilities. That's great if you want to become an AI expert.

But you don't need to be an AI expert. You need to solve YOUR specific problem - whatever's wasting your time right now.

This skips the theory and goes straight to: "Here's what to do Monday morning at YOUR company with YOUR tools and YOUR budget." It's like the difference between taking a cooking class vs. getting a step by step recipe for tonight's dinner, crafted for you.

Do I need to know how to code?

‚ñº

Nope! Not at all.

Most AI implementation today uses no-code tools (things you can set up by clicking buttons, not writing code). If you can use Google Sheets or send an email, you can do this.

The solutions we recommend are based on your tech level - so if you said "beginner," we won't tell you to build a Python script. We'll show you tools with simple interfaces.

Will this actually work for my industry?

‚ñº

Honestly? Some industries are easier than others right now.

AI is GREAT at: data entry, scheduling, email drafting, invoice tracking, customer service responses, content writing, basic reporting.

AI is still LEARNING: complex physical tasks, highly regulated decisions (medical diagnoses, legal rulings), anything requiring human judgment on ethical gray areas.

If your pain point involves repetitive digital tasks (emails, spreadsheets, scheduling, data), then yes - very likely to work. If it's "AI should diagnose my patients," then no - not yet (and possibly never without human oversight).

Industries where AI is already helping:

  • Retail: Inventory tracking, customer service emails
  • Restaurants: Staff scheduling, food ordering
  • Healthcare: Appointment reminders, insurance paperwork
  • Construction: Project estimates, client follow-ups
  • Real Estate: Lead follow-up, contract prep
  • Professional Services: Time tracking, client communications

How much will this actually cost me?

‚ñº

Depends on what you choose! Here's the reality:

  • Free option: Use free ChatGPT + free tools like Google Sheets, Zapier free tier. Cost = $0
  • Budget option: ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) + one automation tool ($10-30/month) = $30-50/month
  • Enterprise-level: Top-tier AI toolkit = $100-200/month

But here's the key: if you're saving 10 hours a week of someone's time, even the $200/month option pays for itself instantly. A $50k/year employee costs roughly $25/hour, so 10 hours saved = $250/week = $1,000/month saved.

That's why the "boss pitch" feature calculates this - to show it's worth it.

What if my boss says no?

‚ñº

Two approaches:

1. Start small/free - Use free tools first, prove it works on a small scale, THEN ask for budget. Hard to say no to something that's already saving time.

2. Make it a no-brainer - The pitch generator shows them the math: "We spend $X, we save $Y." If Y is way bigger than X, it's hard to say no.

Worst case? You still learned something valuable, you can use these skills at your next job, and you become "the person who knows AI" - which is a resume booster right now.

How long does implementation take?

‚ñº

Real talk:

  • Getting the plan: 5 minutes (literally, just fill out the form)
  • Setting up basic automation: 1-4 hours for simple stuff (depends on your tech level)
  • Testing and tweaking: 1-2 weeks to make sure it actually works
  • Full rollout: 1-2 months if you're changing how your whole team works

But you'll start seeing benefits within days, not months. Even a half-working solution that saves you 2 hours a week is worth it while you refine it.

Can I really do this myself, or do I need to hire someone?

‚ñº

You can absolutely do this yourself - that's the whole point!

But let's be honest about what "yourself" means:

  • You'll need to spend a few hours learning and setting things up
  • You might hit roadblocks - just use the AI Helper button to get instant support
  • Some trial and error is normal

If you're the type of person who figured out how to use Zoom during COVID, or learned how to use a new app at work without help - you can do this.

If you genuinely hate technology and avoid learning new things - you might want to partner with a more tech-savvy coworker or hire someone. But try it first! You might surprise yourself.

What if I try this and it doesn't work?

‚ñº

Then you learned something! Seriously.

Not every solution works perfectly the first time. Maybe the AI tool you picked isn't quite right, or your process needs adjusting. That's normal.

The difference between people who succeed with AI and people who don't isn't intelligence - it's persistence. The successful ones try something, see what needs tweaking, adjust, and try again.

Plus, most AI tools have free trials or cheap monthly plans - so if something doesn't work, you're out maybe $20 and a few hours, not thousands of dollars. Don't forget you have the chat tool to assist along the way.

Is this going to replace my job?

‚ñº

No - it's going to make you BETTER at your job and more valuable.

Think about it: the person who figures out how to save the company 20 hours a week isn't getting fired - they're getting promoted (or at least a reputation as the person who Gets Stuff Done).

AI replaces tasks, not people who solve problems. By learning to implement AI, you become the problem-solver, not the task-doer.

What if something goes wrong?

‚ñº

Start small! This is the golden rule of implementing anything new at work.

Here's how to stay safe:

  • Test on yourself first - Don't roll out to your whole team until you've tried it yourself for at least a week
  • Make backups - Before changing any existing systems, save copies of what you have now
  • Get manager approval - Show them the plan, explain the benefits, get the green light before implementing
  • Start with one small process - Don't automate everything at once. Pick one thing, prove it works, then expand
  • You can always undo it - Most AI tools are just software you can turn off if they're not working

Remember: AI Hero provides guidance and education. You're responsible for testing and implementing safely in your specific environment. When in doubt, start smaller than you think you need to.
